# "The Great Old Ones" Total Conversion Roadmap

## Overview
"The Great Old Ones" transforms Vector War into a Lovecraftian horror strategy experience. Players command the Esoteric Order, a shadowy cabal seeking to awaken ancient cosmic entities and usher in an age of madness. The roadmap structures development across four phases that layer distinct awakening doctrines, human sanity systems, and eldritch escalation loops. Victory comes through conquest, corruption, or cosmic convergence‚Äîbut the cost may be humanity's collective mind.

## üé≠ Campaign Mode Packaging

Leveraging the Phase 1 **Mission Generator** foundations and the Phase 3 **Dynamic Campaign Generator**, the Great Old Ones campaign is packaged into three escalating acts. Each act stitches authored story beats to procedural objectives so narrative teams can scope bespoke encounters while systems dynamically remix investigation pacing.

- **Act I ‚Äì The Gathering (7 missions)**
  - Mission mix: 4 authored establishment missions (found first cells, establish ritual sites, recruit key cultists), 3 generated harvesting/recruitment ops from Mission Generator
  - Unlock trigger: Available at campaign start; Act II unlocks once three regions have corruption index above 30% and the High Priest council ratifies initial doctrine alignment
  - **Story Beats**:
    - The Founding Vision: Player character receives first contact with eldritch entity
    - Recruitment of the Inner Circle: Gather five unique high priest characters
    - First Blood Ritual: Inaugural major working establishing credibility
    - The Investigator Appears: Introduction of recurring detective antagonist

- **Act II ‚Äì The Awakening (9 missions)**
  - Mission mix: 5 scripted escalation flashpoints (major summoning attempts, infiltration of key governments, sanity crisis management), 4 adaptive corruption/ritual sorties seeded by Mission Generator using regional sanity weights
  - Unlock trigger: Requires completion of Act I, plus at least one Dynamic Campaign Generator branch (e.g., investigator alliance formation or rival cult emergence) and sustained eldritch power accumulation for five consecutive turns
  - **Story Beats**:
    - The First Manifestation: Successful summoning visible to public, media frenzy
    - The Great Schism: Internal Order split over methods or goals
    - The Celebrity Convert: High-profile public figure joins Order, massive influence boost
    - The Investigator Strikes: Major raid on a ritual site, casualties on both sides
    - The Nightmare Plague: Mass sanity event affecting entire city/region

- **Act III ‚Äì The Convergence (6 missions + finale)**
  - Mission mix: 4 bespoke finale lead-ins tied to motivation reveal chains, 2 dynamically selected counter-occult operations, and a branching final mission authored per doctrine path
  - Unlock trigger: Opens when Dynamic Campaign Generator flags both an investigation escalation and an internal Order crisis, forcing players to resolve a loyalty vote before progressing
  - **Story Beats**:
    - The Truth Revealed: Player learns the actual nature of the entities they serve
    - The Final Sacrifice: Choice to sacrifice inner circle for power or find alternative
    - The Global Response: Coordinated international counter-offensive
    - The Rival's Gambit: Competing cult attempts to awaken their patron first
    - **Branching Finales**:
      - Domination: "The Stars Are Right" - Cthulhu rises from R'lyeh
      - Corruption: "The New Order" - Corrupted UN votes to serve the Old Ones
      - Convergence: "The Threshold" - Humanity voluntarily crosses into cosmic enlightenment

### Campaign Completion Acceptance Criteria

- **Act Gates**: Players must clear all missions in each act and meet unlock triggers before subsequent acts spawn; mission generator retries cannot bypass High Priest votes or corruption thresholds
- **High Priest Votes**: Final act requires unanimous council approval or passed emergency override (2/3 majority plus entity favor) to greenlight finale mission, recording outcome for save-state continuity
- **Corruption/Sanity Thresholds**:
  - Campaign completion flags only if global corruption averages above 50% OR global sanity below 40%
  - For Convergence doctrine, requires Voluntary Conversion rate exceeding 60%
  - Exceeding inverse thresholds (too much resistance) branches into Investigator Victory end state requiring resolution before credits
- **Save-State Hooks**: Mission completions, vote tallies, corruption averages, summoned entities, and fallen investigators written to dynamic campaign state tree for scripting team to author re-entry beats and NG+ unlocks
- **Entity Favor Tracking**: Each Old One patron tracks player actions; favor score affects finale options and post-game outcomes

## üé® Unique Mechanical Innovations

### The Madness Contagion System
Building on Vector War's plague mechanics, madness spreads through:
- **Mental Transmission**: Dreams, visions, psychic contact
- **Social Transmission**: Cult recruitment, word of mouth, witness testimony
- **Media Transmission**: Books, films, art, music, internet memes
- **Environmental Transmission**: Proximity to ritual sites, eldritch artifacts, summoned entities

Each strain has evolution paths balancing:
- **Visibility vs. Stealth**: Obvious madness triggers investigation, subtle corruption spreads further
- **Lethality vs. Conversion**: Dead minds provide sanity fragments, living cultists provide utility
- **Euphoria vs. Terror**: Happy victims spread madness willingly, terrified victims alert authorities

### The Cosmic Alignment Calendar
Ritual effectiveness tied to celestial events:
- **Daily**: Lunar phase modifiers
- **Monthly**: Planetary alignments
- **Seasonal**: Solstices and equinoxes
- **Rare**: Eclipses, comets, stellar conjunctions
- **Legendary**: "Stars Are Right" events enabling Great Old One awakenings

Players must plan major workings around astronomy, creating strategic timing decisions.

### The Veil Integrity System
Replaces traditional stealth with cosmic horror themes:
- **High Veil (80-100%)**: Order operates in complete secrecy, minimal investigation pressure
- **Medium Veil (40-79%)**: Rumors circulate, investigators probe, authorities skeptical but alert
- **Low Veil (10-39%)**: Public awareness of supernatural, panic spreading, organized resistance forming
- **Torn Veil (0-9%)**: Open supernatural warfare, humanity united against Order, emergency powers enacted
- **Shattered Veil (negative)**: Reality itself unstable, physics breaking down, entities manifesting freely

Different doctrines optimize for different veil states:
- **Domination**: Thrives at low veil (fear is the weapon)
- **Corruption**: Requires medium-high veil (shadows enable infiltration)
- **Convergence**: Benefits from controlled veil lowering (gradual revelation)

### The Entity Hierarchy
Five tiers of summonable beings:
1. **Servitors**: Weak but expendable, minimal cost, low backlash risk
2. **Horrors**: Combat-capable, moderate cost, requires binding circles
3. **Star Spawn**: Powerful entities, high cost, significant backlash if uncontrolled
4. **Avatars**: Physical manifestations of Old Ones, extreme cost, regional disasters if control fails
5. **Great Old Ones**: Campaign-ending summonings, require multi-turn rituals, reshape reality permanently

Each tier has specific uses, risks, and strategic implications.

### The Sanity Economy
Dual resource system balancing:
- **Sanity Fragments** (harvested from broken minds): Liquid resource for rituals and summonings
- **Eldritch Power** (accumulated through workings): Battery resource decaying over time

Players must balance:
- **Harvesting vs. Converting**: Extract sanity or recruit cultists
- **Spending vs. Storing**: Use power immediately or save for major workings
- **Speed vs. Stealth**: Fast operations raise suspicion, slow ones risk investigator interference

Creating strategic depth in resource management.

## üåä Thematic Pillars

### Cosmic Insignificance
Humans are playthings of vast entities beyond comprehension. Victory may be survival, not triumph.

Are the Old Ones evil destroyers or indifferent cosmic forces? Is awakening them extinction or transcendence? Players decide through actions and interpretation.

### The Price of Power
Every ritual has a cost. Every summoning risks disaster. Every conversion alienates the unconverted. Expansion requires sacrifice.
